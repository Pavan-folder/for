# **Project Overview**

**CuraLink** is an AI-powered platform designed to connect **patients**, and **researchers** by simplifying the discovery of **clinical trials**, **medical publications**, and **health experts**.

The goal of this hackathon is to build an **MVP (Minimum Viable Product)** that demonstrates this idea through a **clean, simple, and intuitive UI/UX**. The focus should be on delivering a user experience that feels effortless where both patients and researchers can easily find what matters most to them.

---

## üéØ **What You Need to Do**

**Create an MVP for CuraLink** that includes:

- Minimal landing page
- Separate onboarding for both patients and researchers
- A **personalized dashboard** for both patients and researchers
- **Personalized** clinical trials, health experts, publications, and forums for patients
- **Personalized** clinical trials, collaborators, and forums for researchers
- A **Favorites section** to save and track interesting studies or experts

### **2. üë©‚Äç‚öïÔ∏è For Patients/Caregivers Flow**

### **Step 1: Profile Setup (Patient)**

- **Natural Language Input**:
    - Ask the patient to enter details about themselves (e.g., symptoms, medical conditions).
    - **Example**: ‚ÄúI have Brain Cancer.‚Äù
    - **Note**: The platform begins populating **relevant publications**, **experts**, and **clinical trials** based on the condition provided.
    - Patients enter personal and medical (disease of interest) information to build a tailored profile. Keep it broad and general (dropdown or free text).
    - The input can be in natural language so AI can automatically identify the conditions.
    - **Condition Filters**: Add more conditions like *Glioma* or *Lung Cancer* to further personalize results.
    - **Location Filters**:
        - Enter location details (city, country).
        - **Note**: While the platform shows experts globally, filtering by **proximity** helps narrow results.
        - Users can toggle filters to view **all experts**, regardless of location.

### **Step 2: Dashboard**

- **Personalized Dashboard**:
    - Automatically populate **recommended publications**, **health experts**, and **clinical trials** based on the patient‚Äôs condition and interests.
    - **Feature**: Display the latest **clinical trials**, **publications**, and **experts**.

### **Step 3: Health Experts**

- Patients can search and view recommended **health experts** based on their conditions or diseases (e.g., ‚ÄúGlioma‚Äù).
- If a researcher has a detailed profile, the platform pulls **specialties** and **research interests** from it.
- **Generic Search**: If a researcher isn‚Äôt part of the platform, it will pull data from publications and external sources (this part needs further research to make it functional).
- **Follow/Request Meeting**: Patients can **follow** experts or **request a meeting**.
    - Meeting requests can only be made if the researcher is active on the platform. Otherwise, a manual request goes to the admin.
    - Patients must provide basic details (name, contact) for meeting requests.
    - Option to nudge experts to join.

### **Step 4: Clinical Trials Search**

- **Search Clinical Trials**: Patients can **type keywords** (e.g., ‚ÄúLung Cancer Immunotherapy Trials‚Äù), and it should suggest **recommended trials** based on their interests.
- **Filters**: Trials can be filtered by **recruitment phase** (recruiting, completed) and **location**.
- Example: [ClinicalTrials.gov Study](https://clinicaltrials.gov/study/NCT06562582?cond=heart%20attack&aggFilters=status:not%20rec&rank=1).
- Ideally, a clickable email option should open a compose box to contact trial administrators.
- **AI-generated summaries** are an important feature to simplify clinical trial or publication content.

### **Step 5: Publications**

- **Recommended Publications**: Display a list of relevant publications.
- **Link to Full Papers**: Direct users to the publication‚Äôs website for full access.
- When searching by keywords, specific research papers should appear.

### **Step 6: Forums**

- **Forum Categories**: Spaces for discussions (e.g., *Cancer Research*, *Clinical Trials Insights*).
- Patients can post questions in a Reddit-style format. Only researchers can reply; patients cannot respond to each other.
- Patients cannot create new communities ‚Äî build this feature smartly.

### **Step 7: Favorites**

- **Save Favorites**: Patients can save their favorite **publications**, **clinical trials**, and **experts**.
- **My Favorites** will be a dedicated dashboard section.

### **3. üßë‚Äçüî¨ For Researchers Flow**

### **Step 1: Profile Setup (Researcher)**

**Personal and Professional Information**

Provide background and expertise to connect with relevant opportunities and collaborators.

- **Specialties**: Mention areas like *Oncology*, *Neurology*, or *Immunology* to categorize your profile.
- **Research Interests**: Add areas like *Immunotherapy*, *Clinical AI*, or *Gene Therapy* to receive relevant recommendations.
- Optional links for auto-importing publications:
    - **ORCID**: Auto-import credentials and publications.
    - **ResearchGate**: Pull academic contributions and connect with other researchers.
- Keep these optional; if added, they‚Äôll fetch publications automatically.
- Ensure AI-generated summaries of fetched content are simple and clear.
- Include an option for researchers to indicate availability for meetings.
- Display their publications with AI-generated summaries.

### **Step 2: Researcher Dashboard**

- **Personalized Dashboard**:
    - View **clinical trials**, **potential collaborators**, and manage **forums** by replying to patient questions.

### **Step 3: Collaborators**

- **Search for Collaborators**:
    - View or search global collaborators by **specialty** or **keywords**.
    - Profiles show **research interests** and **recent publications**.
- Researchers can send **connection requests** and **chat** once accepted.

### **Step 4: Manage Clinical Trials**

- **Add Clinical Trials**: Add new trials with details like **eligibility**, **phase**, **status**, and **description**.
- **Manage Existing Trials**: Update recruitment progress and participant numbers.
- **AI-generated Summary**: Set up parameters summarizing research topics and key findings briefly.

### **Step 5: Forums**

- **Engage in Discussions**: Researchers can create communities and lead discussions.
- **Ask/Answer Questions**: Researchers can ask or respond to relevant forum topics.

### **Step 6: Favorites**

- **Save Favorites**: Researchers can save relevant **clinical trials**, **publications**, and **collaborators** for future reference.
